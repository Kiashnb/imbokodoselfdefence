IMBOKODO
SELF DEFENCE

Confidently Empowering our Youth

Our Aim

To support and stand against violence towards women and children, Danika and Diara have created a self-sustaining initiative called “Imbokodo Self Defence”. The aim of Imbokodo Self Defence NPC is to empower as many teenagers as possible and introduce them to basic self-defence so that they can escape a situation that makes them feel uncomfortable.

Concept

The idea behind Imbokodo Self Defence is to reach as many teenagers as possible, from all walks of life, and teach them basic self-defence, as well as self-awareness and awareness of their surroundings. To achieve this concept, a 4-hour programme has been designed by an internationally trained mixed Martial Arts professional. This programme will be taught to teenagers in areas where a charge of R250 is affordable. All proceeds received from these sessions will be used to fund the same sessions to be taught in disadvantaged areas that will not be able to afford this cost. Included in these disadvantaged areas will be safe houses for teenagers, orphanages, youth centres and schools. “When we train you, we will also train another.”

Programme

These sessions are taught by highly skilled, approachable trainers who create a respectful and safe environment for the girls to deal with the realities of their need to be able to defend themselves. To ensure a well- balanced interactive session, the number of trainers will be dependent on the number of students in a class. Although this programme has been designed for teenage girls, it can be adapted to include boys. It can also be adapted for adults if required.


The programme will include:

  • Basic Strikes (how to hold a fist and where to attack on the human body)
  • Defence against an attempted choke hold
  • Defence against a hair grab
  • A hold against hostiles if they throw a punch
  • How to manoeuvre if ever you find yourself on your back (either use the triangle hold or an arm bar)

Funding

This being a nonprofit organisation, all proceeds received from classes are used to host the same classes in disadvantaged areas. Any donations are also welcomed to help us achieve our goal.
